Interdisciplinary Projects & Research

1. AI Chatbot for Philosophy (Capstone)

Overview: Designed a conversational agent that can discuss philosophical systems and provide references. The goal was to support undergraduate learners exploring ethical frameworks and logic.

Methodologies: Fine-tuning language models, prompt engineering, building a small retrieval system for cited sources, and human-in-the-loop evaluation.

Outcomes & innovations: A usable prototype with a curated knowledge base covering major philosophical schools, demonstration scripts, and evaluation metrics for answer relevance and safety.

Challenges & solutions: Ensuring accuracy and preventing hallucinations; addressed via a retrieval layer and explicit citation prompts.

NLPHumanitiesEthics

2. Protein Structure Prediction — Interdisciplinary Study

Overview: Explored deep learning approaches for protein folding prediction inspired by AlphaFold. The project connected computational biology, machine learning, and software engineering.

Methodologies: Data preprocessing for protein sequences, CNNs and attention-based architectures, evaluation using RMSD metrics.

Outcomes: Implemented baseline models and a visualization tool to inspect predicted structures; documented failure modes and areas for improvement.

Challenges & solutions: Data scarcity and compute limits — mitigated by careful sampling, transfer learning, and using lightweight models for prototyping.

MLBioinformaticsVisualization

3. Cryptography Lab Suite (Java)

Overview: Implemented classical ciphers (Playfair, Rail Fence, Gronsfeld) and evaluated them for learning and demonstration purposes. The project combined theory, secure coding practices, and educational tooling.

Methodologies: Algorithm design in Java, unit tests for correctness, and small GUI for inputs and visualizing transformations.

Outcomes: A package of tested cipher implementations used as teaching aids and documented in an accompanying report.
